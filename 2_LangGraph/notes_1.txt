==========LangGraph=========
- Is a powerful framework for orchestarting complex workflows, allowing
you to define sequences of operations(nodes) and how they connect(edges)
to form a complete application.
- Framework handles interactions:
	> Large Language Models(LLMs)
	> External APIs
	> Custom functions----> MCP servers---> Tools
- What to expect:
a. Fundamental concepts of constructing a graph
b. Define state of the graph
c. Adding simple python functions as nodes
d. Connecting them with the edges
e. Setting a starting point
f. Compiling it.
g. Add external tools(MCP Server):
	> TavilySearchResults
	> Weather App
- demonstrate how to wrap a custom function into a tool.


====Core Concepts: Agent's Blueprint====
- Let's understand the fundamental components:
1. Graph: Master plan for your AI agent. Its the blueprint for your
application's workflow, a collection of interconnected nodes and edges
2. Nodes: Are doers or steps in your workflow. Each Node performs a
specific action or represents an AI agent. Nodes are increadibly versative:
	> An LLM call
	> A tool invocation(e.g.m a search API, Calculator)
	> A custom python function
	> Another LangChain runnable
3. Edges: Pathways that connect your nodes, defining how your worklflow
progresses. One node completes its actions, an edge determines which
node executes next.
Types of edges:
	> Conditional Edges: Decision points. Based on ouput the node, the graph
can choose to go down different paths (to different subsequent nodes)
	> Direct Edges: Straightforward connections; the workflow simply moves
from one node directly to the next.
4. State: Individual nodes operate independently, real-world AI apps need
to maintain information throughout a conversation or task. The "graph state"
is shared memory between nodes.
5. Entry Point(START): Special node marks where your graphs execution begins
Think of it as the starting line of your agents journey
6. Finish Point(END): Conclusion of a specific path with your workflow.
